INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

 

CONFIDENTIAL

 

This Independent Contractor Agreement (the “Agreement”) is made by and between {{name}} (“Contractor”) and Profesy Professionals LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”) (collectively the “Parties”) and will be effective the day Contractor signs this Agreement (“Effective Date”). The Parties agree as follows:

 

  1. Term. The initial term of this Agreement shall begin on the Effective Date and will remain in effect for a period of one (1) year.  The term will be extended automatically for an additional year on an annual basis, unless expressly revoked by either Party. Notwithstanding the above, (i) the Company may terminate this Agreement at any time by notice to Contractor for any reason or no reason, and (ii) Contractor may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing 30 days’ written notice to the Company for any reason or no reason.  In the event of any such termination, the Company will be liable for payment for Services, as defined herein, satisfactorily completed in accordance with this Agreement on or prior to the date of termination.

 

  1. Acknowledgements and Independent Contractor Status. It is agreed that, during the term of this Agreement, Contractor’s relationship to the Company will be as an independent contractor, and that neither this Agreement nor the Services to be rendered hereunder shall, for any purpose, create an employer-employee relationship. Contractor will maintain complete control for Contractor’s Services, employees, agents, methods, and operations. Contractor is not and will not purport to be an agent, subsidiary, or affiliate of the Company for any purpose. Contractor is not eligible to participate in any vacation, group medical or life insurance, disability, profit sharing or retirement benefits, plans or distributions related to any compensation plan, insurance programs or other fringe benefits which the Company may provide its employees. Contractor shall assume and accept all responsibilities that are imposed on independent contractors by any law or regulation or otherwise. Contractor shall not have any right, power or authority to create any obligation, express or implied, or make any representation on behalf of the Company, unless an authorized representative of the Company provides Contractor with advance written authorization.

 

  1. Scope of Services and Fee. Contractor shall provide the Company with Services for a fee, as outlined Exhibit A attached hereto.  “Services” means the activities in which Contractor is engaged on for the Company.  In the course of doing so, Contractor shall at all times act in the best interest of the Company. Contractor represents that Contractor possesses capable skills, abilities, and experience with respect to the Services to be provided under this Agreement.  Contractor agrees to carry out all contractual obligations required under this Agreement in a professional, competent and diligent manner, using Contractor’s own equipment.

 

  1. Taxes. The Company is not responsible for paying any social security or withholding tax, or any other similar employment or business tax or expense on behalf of Contractor. Contractor agrees to be exclusively responsible for the payment of all federal, state, and local income taxes (including social security) related to any payments from the Company. Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold the Company harmless against all loss, damage, expense, and liability arising from Contractor’s failure to pay taxes, including penalties and interest. 

 

  1. Non-Disclosure of Confidential Information. Contractor understands that Contractor will have access to the Company’s trade secrets, proprietary and confidential information that has commercial value to the Company and/or is important to its business (“Confidential Information”). Confidential Information is information relating to the Company or its business which is not generally available to the public or ascertainable through common sense or general business or technical knowledge. Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to, customer lists and preferences, employee and contractor lists, business operations and procedures, products, Services, Intellectual Property, methods, strategy, pricing, records, accounts, and creative, financial, marketing or other data embodying or relating to the business of the Company, in each case whether in written, oral, electronic, magnetic, photographic, optical or other forms, and including all Work Product and all ideas, concepts, designs, algorithms, calculations, methods, techniques and know-how.

 

In exchange for having access to Confidential Information, Contractor agrees that, both during and after the term of this Agreement, Contractor will not, directly or indirectly, publish, disclose or use Confidential Information for Contractor’s benefit or for the benefit of any competitor or other third party, except as necessary in properly performing Services on behalf of the Company under the Agreement. Contractor will not make copies of any Confidential Information, except as reasonably necessary to perform the Services or with the express prior written authorization of the Company.

 

Contractor shall use a reasonable degree of care to protect all Confidential Information. Contractor shall notify the Company immediately upon discovery of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information, whether intentional or inadvertent, and will cooperate with Company in every reasonable way to help Company regain possession and maintain the confidentiality of the Confidential Information, and prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure of same.

 

If Contractor is required (by oral questions, interrogatories, requests for information or documents in legal proceedings, subpoena, civil investigative demand or other similar legal process) to disclose any Confidential Information, and unless a court order or applicable law prevents it, Contractor will provide the Company with prompt notice of any such request or requirement so that the Company may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy and/or waive compliance with the provisions of this Agreement. If, in the absence of a protective order or other remedy or the receipt of a waiver by the Company, Contractor is nonetheless legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information to any tribunal, regulatory authority, agency or similar entity, Contractor may disclose, without liability hereunder, only that portion of the Confidential Information which is legally required to be disclosed, provided that Contractor exercises best efforts, including cooperation with the Company, to preserve the confidentiality of the Confidential Information, and provided further that Contractor did not produce the legal compulsion to disclose by taking actions after the date of this Agreement that were not compelled by applicable law. Nothing in this Agreement prohibits Contractor from reporting possible violations of federal law or regulation to any governmental agency or entity, including the Department of Justice, the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Congress, and any agency inspector general, or making other disclosures that are protected under the whistleblower provisions of any applicable law or regulation. Contractor does not need the prior authorization of the Company to make any such reports or disclosures and Contractor is not required to notify the Company that Contractor has made such reports or disclosures.

 

  1. Work Product. The product of all work performed under this Agreement (“Work Product”), including without limitation all notes, contacts, customer lists and preferences, reports, documentation, computer programs, apps, inventions, creations, works, devices, models, works of authorship, concepts, ideas, strategies, inventions, technical data, creative data, software, source code, text, art work, graphics, suggestions, titles, characters, characterizations, designs, design concepts, drawings, information, functional and technical designs and specifications, interfaces, flowcharts, site maps, navigation maps, operating instructions, design concepts, work-in-progress and deliverables which is conceived, made, reduced to practice, or learned by Contractor, solely or in conjunction with others, in the course of any work performed for the Company, will be the sole property of the Company, and Contractor hereby assigns to the Company all right, title and interest therein, including but not limited to all audiovisual, literary, moral rights, and other copyrights, patent rights, trade secret rights and other proprietary rights therein, including those incorporating, based on or derived from the foregoing. Contractor retains no right to use the Work Product and agree not to challenge the validity of the Company’s ownership in the Work Product. Contractor represents, warrants and covenants that all Work Product will be original work of Contractor free of any claims of any third party.

 

Contractor agrees to assign to the Company, and hereby does assign, all of Contractor’s right, title and interest, under any and all laws, whether domestic or foreign, to all Intellectual Property authored, discovered, developed, conceived or reduced to practice by Contractor, whether alone or with others, (a) that embodies or is related to the business of the Company, (b) that results from the Services (including before the Effective Date), or (c) that results from the use of the Company’s premises or property (including computer systems, engineering facilities, Intellectual Property, servers accessed remotely and software owned, leased, licensed or otherwise contracted for by the Company), including all Work Product (“Company IP”). Contractor hereby waives all moral rights with respect to the Company IP, all rights of identification of authorship and any and all rights of approval, restriction or limitation on use or subsequent modifications under any and all laws, domestic or foreign and whether now existing or hereafter enacted. Contractor agrees, on Contractor’s own behalf and on behalf of Contractor’s successors-in-interest, heirs, executors, administrators and assigns, not to institute, support, maintain or permit directly or indirectly any litigation or proceedings instituted or maintained on the ground that the Company’s (or its licensees’ or designees’) exercise of the rights granted to the Company in the Company IP in any way constitutes an infringement or violation of any right of Contractor. Contractor hereby acknowledges that the consideration to which Contractor is entitled pursuant to this Agreement includes consideration for the assignment and waiver of rights under this Section 6 and that the said consideration is an equitable and adequate part of the revenues derived or to be derived by the Company from those rights. “Intellectual Property” as used herein means any and all right, title and interest in or relating to intellectual property existing from time to time, whether protected, created or arising under the Laws of the United States or any other jurisdiction, including under patent law, copyright law, moral right law, trade secret law, trademark law, rights of privacy and publicity and any and all other proprietary rights and any and all applications therefor and renewals, extensions and restorations thereof.

 

Contractor acknowledges and agrees that Contractor has no expectation of privacy with respect to the Company’s telecommunications, networking or information processing systems (including files, e-mail messages, and voice messages) and that Contractor's activity and any files or messages on or using any of those systems may be monitored at any time without notice. Contractor further agrees that any property situated on the Company’s premises and owned by the Company, including disks and other storage media, filing cabinets or other work areas, is subject to inspection by Company personnel at any time with or without notice.

  1. Separation. Upon the termination of this Agreement for any reason, or upon written request of the Company, Contractor promptly will discontinue the use of, and will return to the Company or its designee, all Company information, property (as applicable) and all Confidential Information and trade secrets and copies thereof which were furnished to or otherwise came into the possession of Contractor during the term of this Agreement, and any notes, summaries and other documents or materials in any form (whether written, oral, electronic, magnetic, photographic, optical or otherwise), containing information relating to such confidential information or trade secrets, and will certify such return in writing within 3 days. In the event that such items are not so returned, the Company will have the right to charge Contractor for all reasonable damages, costs, attorneys’ fees, and other expenses incurred in searching for, taking, removing and/or recovering such property, including Confidential Information or trade secrets.

 

  1. Conflicts of Interest. By entering into this Agreement Contractor represents and warrants that he or she is not under any contractual obligation which prohibits Contractor from performing Services for the Company. Contractor is expressly free to perform Services for other parties while performing Services for the Company, so long as performance of such Services does not detrimentally impact the Company or the work performed for the Company.

 

  1. Customer Non-Solicitation.  Contractor agrees that during the term of his or her engagement with the Company and for a period of six (6) months thereafter, Contractor will not directly or indirectly on his or her own behalf or on behalf of any other person or entity, except on behalf of the Company, (i) solicit or call upon any customer or client or Prospective Customer of the Company or any of its affiliates to provide or attempt to provide to such customer or Prospective Customer the services provided by the Company and its Affiliates in connection with the business of the Company, or (ii) induce or attempt to induce any customer of the Company or any of its affiliates to cease doing business with or decrease business with the Company or its affiliates (including, without limitation, by making any negative or disparaging statements or communications regarding the Company or its affiliates).  As used herein, “Prospective Customer” shall mean any person or entity to whom the Company or any of its affiliates has sent or delivered a written service proposal or contract or otherwise solicited in connection with the Business of the Company.  

 

  1. Employee/Contractor Non-Solicitation.  Contractor agrees that during the term of his or her employment and for a period of six (6) months thereafter, Contractor will not directly or indirectly on his or her own behalf or on behalf of any other person or entity, recruit, hire or attempt to recruit or hire, induce away from the Company or any of its affiliates or attempt to induce away from the Company or any of its affiliates, directly or by assisting others, any person or entity who is an employee, consultant, or contractor of the Company or any of the affiliates of the Company or that was employed or engaged by the Company within the twelve (12) months prior to the termination of Contractor’s engagement with the Company.  

 

  1. Enforcement.  If, at the time of enforcement of Sections 5, 6, 9, or 10 of this Agreement, a court holds that the restrictions stated herein are unreasonable under circumstances then existing, the parties hereto agree that the maximum permissible period, scope or geographical area reasonable under such circumstances shall be substituted for the stated period, scope or area and that the court shall revise the restrictions contained herein to cover the maximum period, scope and area permitted by law.  Contractor agrees that the covenants contained in Sections 5, 6, 9, and 10 are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company’s legitimate business interests and properties of the Company; and that irreparable loss and damage may be suffered by the Company should Contractor breach any of the covenants.  Therefore, Contractors agrees that in addition to all other remedies provided by law or in equity, the Company and its affiliates or their successors and assigns may seek a temporary restraining or protective order and temporary and permanent injunction to prevent a breach or contemplated breach of any of the covenants (without posting a bond or other security).   Contractor acknowledges that the provisions of Sections 5, 6, 9, and 10 are in consideration of: (i) engagement as a consultant with the Company, and (ii) additional good and valuable consideration as set forth in this Agreement.  Contractor acknowledges that he or she has carefully read this Agreement and have given careful consideration to the restraints imposed upon Contractor by this Agreement and Contractor is in full accord as to their necessity for the reasonable and proper protection of confidential and proprietary information and relationships of the Company and its affiliates now existing or to be developed in the future.  Contractor expressly acknowledges and agrees that each and every restraint imposed by this Agreement is reasonable with respect to subject matter, time, and scope.  

 

  1. Indemnification. As an independent contractor, Contractor is solely responsible for the Services provided to the Company, and neither the Company, nor its customers and clients, shall be liable to any party for the Services provided by Contractor. Contractor shall defend, indemnify and hold the Company, and its employees and agents harmless for any claims, losses, damages, liabilities, deficiencies, actions, judgments, interest, awards, penalties, fines, costs or expenses, alleged or imposed against the Company by any party arising out of or resulting from Contractor’s acts or omissions or breach of this Agreement. This indemnification includes, but is not limited to, claims related to worker’s compensation, disability, bodily injury or death of any person or animal, negligence, property damage, claims that the Services or Work Product infringe any Intellectual Property, breach of contractor, tort, wage and hour laws, employment taxes, employment classification, benefits, or any other matters involving the acts or omissions of Contractor. This indemnification shall be for all loss and shall survive the termination of this Agreement. Indemnification shall not be limited by an amount or a type of damages. 

 

  1. Severability. All provisions, terms, conditions, paragraphs and covenants contained in this Agreement are severable and, in the event any one of them shall be held to be invalid by any court of competent jurisdiction, this Agreement shall be interpreted as if such provision was not contained herein, and such determination shall not otherwise affect the validity of any other provisions.

  2. Choice of Law and Dispute Resolution. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed and enforced in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without giving effect to the conflict of laws principles thereof.  Any claim, action, suit or proceeding that arises from or relates to this Agreement shall be brought and conducted solely and exclusively within the courts of the State of Delaware.  

 

  1. Assignment. Contractor may not assign any of his or her rights under this Agreement, or delegate the performance of any of his or her duties hereunder, without the prior written consent of the Company. The Company may assign its rights under this Agreement.

 

  1. Miscellaneous. If any provision of this Agreement (including the Exhibits attached hereto) is construed to be invalid, illegal or unenforceable, then the remaining provisions hereof shall not be affected thereby and shall be enforceable without regard thereto. This Agreement may be executed counterparts, each of which will be deemed to be an original copy of this Agreement and all of which, when taken together, will be deemed to constitute one and the same agreement. Neither the failure nor delay on the part of either party to exercise any right, remedy, power, or privilege under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof. This Agreement (including the Exhibits attached hereto) represent the entire agreement and understanding of the Parties. Except as otherwise provided herein, this Agreement shall not be amended, supplemented, or terminated except in a writing signed by both Parties.
